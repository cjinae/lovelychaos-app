Living System

Design language for the rest of LovelyChaos.

This system is pulled from the current public site: warm parchment backgrounds, serif-led headlines, restrained coral accents, and operational UI that still feels editorial rather than administrative.

01

Calm, not clinical

Internal tools should inherit the same softness as the marketing site. Use warm neutrals, large breathing room, and avoid dashboard-grey defaults.

02

Editorial hierarchy

Use serif for moments of meaning: page titles, section headlines, metrics. Use DM Sans for controls, body copy, labels, and tables.

03

Signal with restraint

Coral is the only hot color. Reserve it for actions, status emphasis, and small moments of guidance so the interface keeps its composure.

Typography

Use contrast, not clutter

Display / Playfair Display

Ready to make chaos simple?

Serif headlines make the product feel human and premium. Keep them short. They should frame the page, not describe every detail.

Body / DM Sans

Use DM Sans for everything operational: table headers, form labels, helper text, and navigation. It keeps the product readable when the content gets dense.

Uppercase labels should be brief and letter-spaced. Body copy should be airy, around `1.6` to `1.8` line height.

Surface cards should feel soft and substantial.

Use 16-28px radii, warm borders, and subtle shadows. Avoid perfectly flat table-and-form UI.

Patterns

How to compose screens

01

Lead with a hero card

Every major page starts with a headline, short framing copy, and one or two clear actions.

02

Group by decision, not model

Users think in tasks like family setup or conversational follow-up, not database entities.

03

Keep dense data framed

Wrap tables in named cards so operational views still feel part of the same brand system.
